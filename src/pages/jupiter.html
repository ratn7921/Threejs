Key Characteristics

üå™Ô∏è The Great Red Spot

Jupiter's most famous feature is a massive storm larger than Earth that has been raging for centuries. This anticyclonic storm has winds reaching speeds of up to 432 km/h and has been observed since 1831.

üß≤ Powerful Magnetic Field

Jupiter has the strongest magnetic field of any planet in our solar system‚Äînearly 20,000 times stronger than Earth's. This field extends millions of kilometers and traps charged particles, creating intense radiation belts.

üåÄ Atmospheric Composition

Jupiter's atmosphere is primarily composed of hydrogen (89%) and helium (10%), with traces of methane, ammonia, and water vapor. The colorful bands are created by different chemical compounds at various altitudes.

üíç Ring System

Jupiter has a faint ring system discovered in 1979 by Voyager 1. The rings are made of dust particles kicked up by micrometeorite impacts on Jupiter's small inner moons.
