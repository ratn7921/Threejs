♀ VENUS

Earth's Twin Sister - The Hottest Planet in Our Solar System

Surface Temperature

462°C

Hot enough to melt lead! Venus is the hottest planet in our solar system due to its extreme greenhouse effect.

Day Length

243 Earth Days

A day on Venus is longer than its year! It rotates very slowly and backwards compared to most planets.

Distance from Sun

108 Million km

Venus is the second planet from the Sun, about 25% closer than Earth.

Atmospheric Pressure

90x Earth's

The pressure on Venus is equivalent to being 1km underwater on Earth.

Mass

81.5% of Earth

Venus is nearly identical in size and mass to Earth, earning it the nickname "Earth's twin."

Orbital Period

225 Earth Days

Venus completes one orbit around the Sun faster than one rotation on its axis.

Venus Facts

Venus is named after the Roman goddess of love and beauty. It's the brightest natural object in the night sky after the Moon, making it visible even during daylight hours. The planet's surface is hidden beneath thick clouds of sulfuric acid, creating a perpetual greenhouse effect that makes it the hottest planet in our solar system.

Internal Structure

Core

Iron & Nickel

Venus has a metallic core approximately 3,200 km in diameter, similar to Earth's but likely completely molten.

Mantle

Rocky Silicate

The mantle is about 3,000 km thick and composed of hot rock that may still be geologically active.

Crust

50 km thick

Venus has a thick basaltic crust with evidence of extensive volcanic activity and tectonic processes.

Magnetic Field

Very Weak

Venus has no significant magnetic field, possibly due to its slow rotation and lack of a magnetic dynamo.

Surface Features

Volcanic Plains

Over 80% of Venus's surface consists of smooth volcanic plains with evidence of extensive lava flows.

Highland Regions

Two major highland regions: Ishtar Terra and Aphrodite Terra, comparable to Earth's continents.

Impact Craters

Relatively few impact craters due to the thick atmosphere and recent volcanic resurfacing.

Atmospheric Composition

Carbon Dioxide

96.5%

The primary component creating the extreme greenhouse effect that superheats the planet.

Nitrogen

3.5%

The second most abundant gas, similar to Earth's atmosphere but in much smaller proportion.

Sulfuric Acid Clouds

Dense Layer

Thick clouds of concentrated sulfuric acid completely obscure the surface from visible light.

Wind Speed

Up to 360 km/h

Hurricane-force winds in the upper atmosphere, despite very slow surface winds.

Atmospheric Layers

Upper Atmosphere (60-80 km)

Sulfuric acid clouds with temperatures around -40°C and extreme winds.

Middle Atmosphere (20-60 km)

Dense CO₂ atmosphere with increasing temperature and pressure with depth.

Lower Atmosphere (0-20 km)

Extremely hot and dense near the surface, with crushing atmospheric pressure.

Space Exploration Missions

1962

Mariner 2 (USA)

First successful planetary flyby mission, confirmed Venus's extreme surface temperatures and lack of magnetic field.

1970

Venera 7 (USSR)

First successful landing on Venus, transmitted data for 23 minutes before succumbing to the harsh conditions.

1975

Venera 9 & 10 (USSR)

First images from Venus's surface, revealing a rocky, volcanic landscape with good visibility.

1989

Magellan (USA)

Radar mapping mission that revealed Venus's detailed surface features and geological history.

2006

Venus Express (ESA)

Studied Venus's atmosphere and climate, operating until 2014 and providing detailed atmospheric data.

2010

Akatsuki (Japan)

Currently studying Venus's atmosphere and meteorology, focusing on cloud dynamics and atmospheric circulation.

Surface Conditions

Opposite
